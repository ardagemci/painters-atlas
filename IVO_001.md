## 2. Constitutional objective

Pigment's north star is to help people discover, understand, and express their taste in art. The relevant product loop is:

> Discover в†’ Admire в†’ Map в†’ Become в†’ Share

This brief addresses a structural break in that loop. Discovery is richly supported, and Admire is widely available, but the user's route back to the resulting map and stored personal states is less prominent than the atlas taxonomies. At the same time, the global Explore promise names more instruments than its hub visibly contains.

The intended user outcome is:

> From any meaningful point in Pigment, a visitor can understand where they are, identify the kind of relationship being offered, continue into the atlas, and recover the personal record created by their actions.

This objective advances the constitution without adding accounts, social features, new infrastructure, or a broader product identity.

### MIRA alignment

MIRA's accepted outcome is a narrow conditional deployment:

> Let a first-time visitor discover art, admire works, form a local Taste Passport, and understand Pigment as a taste atlas.

IVO accepts that outcome and does not treat the larger navigation proposal in this report as a prerequisite for the current deployment. The immediate IA obligation is to verify that the first-user journey has coherent transitions, persistent state, clear recovery, and no hidden dead end. Global-navigation regrouping, taxonomy relabeling, and new retrieval instruments remain follow-up work unless Claude's evidence demonstrates a critical release failure.

## 5. Accepted structural strengths

The coherence pass must preserve the following:

1. **Connected-object behavior.** Every important page offers meaningful lateral and onward paths.
2. **Multiple legitimate entrances.** Known artist, guided List, open exploration, and personal taste are distinct and valuable motivations.
3. **Editorial Lists as guided walks.** They translate a large atlas into intelligible sequences without flattening the work into taxonomy.
4. **Strong detail-page recovery.** Breadcrumbs, related entities, and вЂњGo nextвЂќ structures prevent most content pages from becoming dead ends.
5. **Art-native personal verbs.** Admire, Seen in person, and Saved for later express distinct relationships to an artwork.
6. **Explicit empty-route recovery.** вЂњBlank canvasвЂќ is concise, contextual, and recoverable.
7. **Typed search results.** Result grouping makes the shared search surface compatible with multiple entity types.

## 6. Entity model

### 6.1 Atlas entities

- **Artist** вЂ” creates artworks; belongs to movements; uses techniques; spans eras; has nation associations; participates in influence, teaching, friendship, rivalry, and partnership relationships.
- **Artwork** вЂ” created by an artist; uses techniques; may belong to a movement; has taste coordinates; may be held by a venue; may appear in Lists and the daily feature; accepts personal states.
- **Movement** вЂ” groups artists and works; has parent, branch, sibling, reaction, and chronological relationships; associates with techniques.
- **Technique** вЂ” describes material or procedural practice; connects artists, movements, and works.
- **Era** вЂ” currently implemented as century-oriented temporal grouping.
- **Nation** вЂ” groups artists geographically or culturally; its exact affiliation rule requires explicit definition.
- **Museum or venue** вЂ” physically locates artworks and groups collection and travel discovery.
- **Editorial List** вЂ” orders artworks into an authored interpretive journey.
- **Relationship** вЂ” connects artists through documented teaching, influence, friendship, rivalry, or partnership.

### 6.2 Personal entities and states

- **Taste Passport** вЂ” the persistent personal container.
- **Palette** вЂ” four art-named tones associated with the Passport.
- **Taste map** вЂ” visualization of taste position and secondary signals.
- **Pigment Persona** вЂ” expressive, provisional interpretation of taste; never a diagnosis.
- **Admiration** вЂ” positive artwork taste signal.
- **Seen in person** вЂ” factual encounter log, independent of admiration.
- **Saved for later** вЂ” interest or reading-list state, independent of admiration.
- **Discovery ring** вЂ” recommendation grouping derived from the Passport.

### 6.3 Boundary decisions

1. Admire remains artwork-only in the current phase.
2. Seen and Saved must not silently affect taste coordinates.
3. Artwork is a first-class entity even if an all-Artworks index remains deferred.
4. Movement, Technique, Era, and Nation are classification paths, not four unrelated collections.
5. Explore instruments are projections over the same atlas, not new content entities.

- Reduce top-level choices to product goals.

**Benefit:** Clearer conceptual hierarchy and lower equal-weight choice burden.

- Change the Explore and search wording without altering retrieval paths.

**Benefit:** Smallest release risk.  
**Cost:** Leaves personal-state recovery and Taste findability unresolved. Not sufficient as the complete response.

## 16. Risks

### Primary risks

1. **Copy-only repair:** correcting promises without repairing retrieval would conceal rather than solve the personal-state problem.
2. **Premature navigation redesign:** regrouping all global navigation without evidence may slow expert access and create new responsive or accessibility failures.
3. **Passport overload:** exposing all personal states without hierarchy could turn Taste into a utility dashboard and weaken its identity value.
4. **Frozen-term drift:** well-intended simplification could conflict with accepted product language and stored schema.
5. **Taxonomic overconfidence:** Nation and Movement structures may imply a certainty or singular affiliation that art history does not support.

### Evidence risks

1. The live review did not perform destructive or state-resetting interactions.
2. Onboarding resume and correction behavior was not exercised.
3. Absence of graph nodes from the sampled semantic DOM is not proof that the visualization is inaccessible; independent accessibility testing is required.
4. No analytics or user research proves that eight header choices confuse users or that Taste is undiscoverable.
5. Current collection counts are dated observations, not claims of comprehensiveness or representativeness.
