Girl with a Pearl Earring

Johannes Vermeer · c. 1665 · Mauritshuis, The Hague · 44.5 × 39 cm

Not a portrait — a 'tronie', a study of a type, a face turning toward you or away, forever mid-decision. The background was once deep green, now aged to black, which only sharpens the trick: she exists nowhere, lit by nothing, looking at you specifically. The famous pearl is five or six strokes of paint and probably isn't even a pearl.

  • The 'pearl' has no hook, no ear contact — it floats
  • Her lips are parted just enough to be about to speak
  • There are no eyebrows; nobody notices until told
