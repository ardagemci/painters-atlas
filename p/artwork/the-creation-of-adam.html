The Creation of Adam

Michelangelo Buonarroti · 1511–12 · Sistine Chapel, Vatican City · 280 × 570 cm

God hurtles in on a cloak of angels; Adam reclines, finished but not yet started — and between two index fingers sits the most famous gap in art. The spark that jumps it is withheld forever. Anatomists keep pointing out that God's billowing cloak traces a cross-section of the human brain with uncanny accuracy — coincidence, or the lifelong dissector's signature; Michelangelo isn't telling. Adam borrows his pose from ancient river gods; the immortal idea is the inch of empty plaster.

  • The fingers do not touch — the gap is the subject
  • God's cloak outlines a human brain; decide for yourself
  • Adam's finger hangs limp, God's is charged: read the current's direction
