View of Toledo

El Greco · c. 1599 · The Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York · 121.3 × 108.6 cm

The first great landscape of Spanish art and still its strangest: Toledo under a sky that has already decided something terrible or magnificent, the hills lit green-silver as if by lightning held in reserve. El Greco moved buildings where composition demanded — the cathedral shifted, the alcázar looming — because this is not topography; it is the city's soul under weather. Three centuries on, Cézanne and the Expressionists recognized a colleague.

  • The skyline is rearranged — Toledo never looked like this from anywhere
  • There are no people; the drama is entirely sky against hill
  • Nocturnal green under storm-noon light: impossible, and deliberate
