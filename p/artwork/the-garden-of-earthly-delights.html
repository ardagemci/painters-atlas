The Garden of Earthly Delights

Hieronymus Bosch · c. 1490–1510 · Museo del Prado, Madrid · 205.5 × 384.9 cm (open)

Three panels, one slide: Eden on the left, a naked carnival of strawberry-eating humanity in the middle, and on the right a hell where musical instruments become torture devices and a bird-headed thing eats sinners whole. Painted five centuries before Surrealism, owned by the most Catholic king in Europe, and still the strangest object in any museum that holds it.

  • The hell panel contains music written on a sinner's backside
  • Closed, the shutters show the world on day three — in grey
  • Nobody in the centre panel is older than about twenty-five
