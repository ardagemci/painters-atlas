The Art of Painting

Johannes Vermeer · c. 1666–68 · Kunsthistorisches Museum, Vienna · 120 × 100 cm

Vermeer's largest and most deliberate interior: a painter — his back forever turned — works at a model dressed as Clio, muse of history, before a great creased map of the Netherlands. It is a quiet manifesto about what painting can claim: history, geography, glory, all staged in one Delft room. He never sold it, not even in debt with eleven children. Three centuries later Hitler bought it for his planned museum; it came home to Vienna from a salt mine.

  • The painter begins with the laurel crown — glory painted first
  • The map behind has a crease running through the Netherlands
  • You never see his face; painting keeps its back to you
