Portrait of Juan de Pareja

Diego Velázquez · 1650 · The Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York · 81.3 × 69.9 cm

In Rome to paint the Pope, Velázquez warmed up by painting his enslaved workshop assistant — and the warm-up became the masterpiece. Juan de Pareja looks back with level, unapologetic ease. When it was exhibited at the Pantheon, one witness reportedly said that everything else looked like painting, but this alone looked like truth. Months later Velázquez signed the document freeing Pareja, who went on to paint in his own right.

  • Pareja's gaze: level, unhurried, unforgettable
  • The lace collar is a handful of loose strokes — step back
  • Grey on grey on olive; the face carries all the color
