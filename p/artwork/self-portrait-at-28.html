Self-Portrait at Twenty-Eight

Albrecht Dürer · 1500 · Alte Pinakothek, Munich · 67.1 × 48.9 cm

No artist before had painted himself like this: full-frontal, symmetrical, long-haired, hand raised toward the chest — a pose and gaze that a thousand years of art had reserved for Christ. It is either blasphemy or a thesis, and Dürer meant the thesis: the artist as creator, made in God's image, signing in Latin that he painted himself 'thus, with undying colors,' at twenty-eight. Nuremberg kept the panel. The swagger has never aged a day.

  • The pose is Christ's — frontal, iconic, entirely deliberate
  • The Latin inscription and monogram float like a legal seal
  • Every strand of fur is separate; the point is demonstration
