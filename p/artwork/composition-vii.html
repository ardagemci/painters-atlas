Composition VII

Wassily Kandinsky · 1913 · Tretyakov Gallery, Moscow · 200.6 × 302.2 cm

Over thirty studies, then four days of painting: a three-metre apocalypse with no objects left in it — only collisions of colour that Kandinsky, who heard hues as sounds, composed like a symphony about the Flood and the Resurrection at once. Stand close and it's chaos; step back and a vortex organizes everything around a small blue-violet centre, like an eye of a storm that believes in heaven.

  • Find the dark oval left of centre — everything orbits it
  • No up or down survives; the canvas works rotated (he checked)
  • Thirty-plus studies exist for a painting of 'improvisation'
