The Sick Child

Edvard Munch · 1885–86 · Nasjonalmuseet, Oslo · 120 × 118.5 cm

Munch's sister Sophie died of tuberculosis at fifteen; a decade later he painted the sickroom back into existence — his aunt Karen bowed at the bedside, a hired model in Sophie's pillow. He scraped and repainted the canvas for a year until the surface looked as wounded as the memory. Critics at the 1886 Autumn Exhibition jeered at the 'half-rubbed-out sketch.' Munch never budged: most of what he later did, he said, was born in this picture.

  • The surface is scratched and scored — he attacked it with the brush's end
  • The mother's head is bowed; it's the dying child who consoles
  • The pillow dissolves toward halo — memory eating the detail
