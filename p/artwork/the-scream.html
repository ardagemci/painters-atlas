The Scream

Edvard Munch · 1893 · Nasjonalmuseet, Oslo · 91 × 73.5 cm

Munch was walking at sunset when, he wrote, he sensed 'a scream passing through nature' — and painted the sensation, not the scene. The figure isn't screaming; it's hearing the scream and holding its skull shut. The sky's blood-red waves may echo the Krakatoa sunsets that reached Norway. Painted on cheap cardboard, it became the modern face of anxiety — and a hidden pencil note calls its maker mad.

  • The two figures behind keep walking — that's the horror
  • The fjord's lines bend to match the skull's curve
  • Look close: it's cardboard, and the paint is thin as panic
