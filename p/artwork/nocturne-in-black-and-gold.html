Nocturne in Black and Gold — The Falling Rocket

James McNeill Whistler · c. 1875 · Detroit Institute of Arts, Detroit · 60.3 × 46.6 cm

Fireworks over Cremorne Gardens dissolved into falling sparks on smoke-blue darkness — a painting of night itself, titled like a piece of music. John Ruskin accused Whistler of 'flinging a pot of paint in the public's face'; Whistler sued for libel and won exactly one farthing, a verdict that bankrupted him and immortalized the argument. Asked in court how long the picture took, he answered: two days — 'but the knowledge of a lifetime.'

  • Three ghost-figures watch from the lower edge — find them
  • The rocket is a scatter of gold flecks: abstraction, in 1875
  • Titled like music on purpose — subject demoted, sensation promoted
