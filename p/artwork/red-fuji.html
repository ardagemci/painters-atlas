Fine Wind, Clear Morning (Red Fuji)

Katsushika Hokusai · c. 1830–32 · The Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York · 25.7 × 38 cm (ōban print)

At dawn in late summer, when the wind is southerly and the sky is clear, Fuji's flank can flush red — and Hokusai compressed that rare minute into three colors and one triangle. After the roaring Wave, the stillness is the point: no people, no story, no waves, just the mountain with the last snow streaking its shoulder under mackerel clouds. It may be the most reproduced landscape print on earth, and it is nearly abstract if you let it be.

  • Three colors do everything: blue, white, and that red
  • The snow runs in rivulets — the mountain's white veins
  • No human figure anywhere — rare for Hokusai, and it's louder for it
