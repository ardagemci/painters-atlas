The Potato Eaters

Vincent van Gogh · 1885 · Van Gogh Museum, Amsterdam · 82 × 114 cm

Van Gogh's first bid for a masterpiece: five peasants around one dish of potatoes, lit by a single lamp, in colors he compared to a dusty, unpeeled potato. He wanted you to smell the earth — the hands that dug the food now reaching for it. A painter friend tore the anatomy apart in a letter; Vincent defended every 'mistake' as truth and the friendship never recovered. To the end of his life he counted it among his best things.

  • Count the light sources: one lamp does everything
  • The hands are painted rougher than the faces — on purpose
  • Steam rises off the dish — the only comfort in the room
