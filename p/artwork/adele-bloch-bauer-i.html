Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I

Gustav Klimt · 1907 · Neue Galerie, New York · 140 × 140 cm

Klimt spent three years and over a hundred preparatory drawings on the Viennese salon hostess Adele Bloch-Bauer, then dissolved her into a wall of gold — face and hands left human, everything else going Byzantine mosaic. The Nazis looted it in 1938 and Austria kept it after the war, renamed 'The Woman in Gold' to erase the Jewish sitter. Her niece Maria Altmann fought for eight years, up to the U.S. Supreme Court, and in 2006 Adele crossed to New York.

  • Only the face and hands are flesh; the dress is already mosaic
  • Little eyes hide among the gold ornaments of her gown — find three
  • The choker reappears in Judith I — same jewels, reportedly the same sitter
