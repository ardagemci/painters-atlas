Stacks of Wheat (End of Summer)

Claude Monet · 1890–91 · The Art Institute of Chicago, Chicago · 60 × 100.5 cm

Monet noticed the light changing faster than he could paint, so he ordered more canvases and worked them in relay — the stack stood still while time became the subject. Twenty-five grainstacks: frost-pink dawns, blue snow, this smoldering end of summer. When fifteen hung together in Paris in 1891, they sold within days at record prices. A few years on, in Moscow, a young lawyer named Kandinsky stood before one, failed to recognize the object — and took it as permission.

  • The shadow is violet and orange — no brown anywhere
  • The stack's outline wobbles: that's heat, not carelessness
  • One canvas implies all the others — the series is the artwork
