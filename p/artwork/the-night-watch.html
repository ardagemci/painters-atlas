The Night Watch

Rembrandt van Rijn · 1642 · Rijksmuseum, Amsterdam · 363 × 437 cm

Its real name is The Company of Captain Frans Banninck Cocq; the 'night' is centuries of darkened varnish. Every other militia portrait lined its members up like a class photo — Rembrandt set them mid-stride, drum beating, muskets in three stages of firing, a golden girl glowing in the crowd with a chicken's claws at her belt, the company emblem. Each man paid to be in it; the ones shoved into shadow reportedly grumbled. It made group portraiture theatre.

  • The girl in gold carries a dead chicken — the militia's claw emblem
  • The captain's open hand casts a real shadow on his lieutenant's coat
  • Find the three muskets: loading, firing, clearing — a manual in one frame
