The Beheading of Saint John the Baptist

Caravaggio · 1608 · St John's Co-Cathedral, Valletta · 361 × 520 cm

The largest canvas Caravaggio ever painted, and the only one he ever signed — his name traced in the blood spilling from the Baptist's throat: 'f. michelangelo,' brother Michelangelo, the fugitive murderer remade as a Knight of Malta. The execution is nearly over; the executioner reaches back for a knife to finish the job. Half the picture is empty prison wall and darkness, and two inmates watch through the bars. It still hangs where he painted it, in the oratory at Valletta.

  • The signature in the Baptist's blood — the only one of his life
  • Two prisoners watch from the barred window at right
  • Half the canvas is empty darkness — silence at mural scale
