The Basket of Apples

Paul Cézanne · c. 1893 · The Art Institute of Chicago, Chicago · 65 × 80 cm

The table tilts, the basket leans, the bottle stands off-plumb, and the biscuits stack at an angle the tabletop cannot explain — every object obeys its own viewpoint, and the still life somehow holds together. That deliberate wrongness is the door Cubism walked through a decade later. 'With an apple I will astonish Paris,' Cézanne reportedly promised. He did it with a basketful, painted so slowly that real fruit rotted and, the story goes, wax stand-ins finished the sitting.

  • Trace the table's front edge: the two halves don't meet
  • Each object has its own eye level — count the viewpoints
  • It should all slide off — feel how the tilts cancel each other
