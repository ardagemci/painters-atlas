The Fighting Temeraire

J.M.W. Turner · 1839 · The National Gallery, London · 90.7 × 121.6 cm

The old warship that fought at Trafalgar is towed up the Thames by a squat steam tug, to be broken up for scrap — sail hauled away by steam, one century hauled off by the next. Turner painted the ship as a pale ghost under a sunset he moved to the wrong compass point because the picture needed it there. He called it his darling, refused every offer to sell, and left it to the nation. Britain has voted it the country's favourite painting.

  • The tug is small, dark and real; the ship is already a ghost
  • The sunset is on the wrong side of the sky — moved for effect
  • Opposite the sun, a sliver of cold moon: the new age's light
