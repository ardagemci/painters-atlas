Mona Lisa

Leonardo da Vinci · c. 1503–19 · Musée du Louvre, Paris · 77 × 53 cm

Leonardo carried her for sixteen years, adding glaze upon glaze until the corners of her mouth and eyes dissolved into smoke — sfumato — precisely where a smile lives or dies. That's the trick: your eye finishes the expression, so she responds to you. A merchant's wife, never delivered to the client, stolen in 1911, and now viewed for an average of fifteen seconds through glass.

  • The smile changes if you stare at her eyes instead
  • The horizon on the left sits lower than the right
  • No eyebrows — fashion, over-cleaning, or both; the debate lives
