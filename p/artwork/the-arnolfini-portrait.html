The Arnolfini Portrait

Jan van Eyck · 1434 · The National Gallery, London · 82.2 × 60 cm

A Bruges merchant and his wife stand in their bedroom among conspicuous possessions — oranges, a brass chandelier, a small indignant dog — while the convex mirror behind them reflects the entire room, two extra visitors included. Above it, in legal script: 'Jan van Eyck was here, 1434.' Wedding certificate, memorial, or flex? Six centuries of argument, zero resolution, one immortal dog.

  • The mirror reflects two visitors — one may be the painter
  • The graffiti above it is a signature posing as testimony
  • Every object is arguing something: oranges, clogs, one candle
