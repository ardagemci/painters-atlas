The Burial of the Count of Orgaz

El Greco · 1586–88 · Iglesia de Santo Tomé, Toledo · 480 × 360 cm

Toledo's parish miracle at civic scale: as the pious count is lowered into his tomb, Saints Stephen and Augustine descend in gold to do the lowering themselves, while above, in a sky of cloud-flesh, the count's soul rises like smoke toward the court of heaven. Below stands a line of black-clad Toledo gentlemen — real, named, ruffed — watching with lawyerly calm. Earth is a portrait gallery; heaven is pure El Greco weather. It has never left the wall it was painted for.

  • The boy pointing at the miracle is El Greco's son — the signature is on his handkerchief
  • Count the white ruffs: Toledo's establishment, painted from life
  • The soul rises as a translucent infant through a birth-canal of cloud
