Madonna

Edvard Munch · 1894–95 · Munch Museum, Oslo · 90 × 68.5 cm

A Madonna at the moment no altarpiece dared paint: conception, ecstasy and death fused in one figure, the halo red instead of gold. The pose reportedly owes to Dagny Juel, the writer at the magnetic centre of Munch's Berlin circle. In the lithograph version a fetus crouches in the frame's corner among swimming sperm — and Munch meant every version to be read that way: the instant life begins, witnessed with equal awe and terror.

  • The halo is red, not gold
  • Her eyes are closed — ecstasy and death-mask at once
  • The aura's wave-lines are the Scream sky, slowed down
