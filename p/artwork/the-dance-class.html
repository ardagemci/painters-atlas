The Dance Class

Edgar Degas · 1874 · Musée d'Orsay, Paris · 85 × 75 cm

Degas haunted the Paris Opéra's rehearsal rooms, not its stages: here the old ballet master Jules Perrot taps his stick while two dozen girls stretch, scratch, yawn and fuss with ribbons — labor, not spectacle. A dog wanders the parquet. The composition tilts like a snapshot a decade before snapshots, figures sliced casually by the frame. Degas called himself a realist, never an Impressionist: what he shared with them was modern life; what he kept was drawing.

  • Nobody is dancing — the subject is waiting, fidgeting, work
  • A girl on the piano scratches her back; another adjusts an earring
  • The floorboards' perspective does the composing — follow them
