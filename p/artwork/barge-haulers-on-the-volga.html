Barge Haulers on the Volga

Ilya Repin · 1870–73 · State Russian Museum, St Petersburg · 131.5 × 281 cm

Eleven men in harness drag a ship against the Volga's current — each one a studied portrait, led by Kanin, a defrocked priest whose philosopher's face Repin found on a sketching trip. The twenty-something painter balanced indictment and monument: the boy at the centre lifts his strap as if about to shrug off the whole empire. Dostoevsky praised the picture for preaching nothing. It became Russian realism's flagship — and, ironically, the Tsar's son bought it.

  • Eleven faces, eleven biographies — start with Kanin at the front
  • The boy in the centre is the only one fighting the harness
  • A tiny steamship on the horizon: the future, ignoring them
