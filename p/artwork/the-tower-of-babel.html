The Tower of Babel

Pieter Bruegel the Elder · 1563 · Kunsthistorisches Museum, Vienna · 114 × 155 cm

Bruegel builds the Bible's doomed skyscraper with Flemish engineering: cranes, ramps, mortar-carriers, a visiting king ignored by his own masons — and a structure already tilting, its courses out of true, clouds sawing off the summit. He had seen the Colosseum in Rome and quotes its arches floor by floor. Awe and warning at once: the most loving portrait of a construction site ever painted, of a project guaranteed to fail.

  • The tower leans — check the horizontal courses against the sea
  • Hundreds of tiny workers; the king is being ignored at lower left
  • Upper floors rise before the lower ones are finished — there's the flaw
