The Third of May 1808

Francisco Goya · 1814 · Museo del Prado, Madrid · 268 × 347 cm

War paintings had always been equestrian ballets about winners. Goya painted an execution by lamplight: a firing squad with no faces, mechanical as a single machine, and a kneeling man in a white shirt with his arms flung wide as a crucifixion. Painted six years after the fact, once the French were finally gone, from testimony and memory. Every modern image of political killing — Manet's Maximilian, Picasso's Guernica — descends from this canvas.

  • The firing squad has no faces; the victims have nothing but
  • One lantern is the only light source — war by lamplight
  • Measure the man in white: kneeling, he's the size of a giant
