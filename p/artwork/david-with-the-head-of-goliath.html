David with the Head of Goliath

Caravaggio · c. 1610 · Galleria Borghese, Rome · 125 × 101 cm

Painted on the run, in his last year — and the severed head David holds up is Caravaggio's own face: swollen, gap-toothed, extinguished. He reportedly sent it to Cardinal Scipione Borghese, the papal nephew with the power to pardon him, which makes the painting a plea: here is my head — spare the rest of me. David is no triumphant hero; he studies his trophy with something close to pity. On the blade, an abbreviation: humility kills pride.

  • Goliath's face is Caravaggio's — a self-portrait as severed head
  • David's expression is pity, not victory
  • The sword's inscription H-AS OS: 'humility slays pride'
