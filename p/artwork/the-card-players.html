The Card Players

Paul Cézanne · c. 1892–95 · Musée d'Orsay, Paris · 47.5 × 57 cm

Farmhands from his family's estate, a bare table, one bottle — and none of the tavern anecdotes the subject had always carried: no cheats, no coins, no onlookers. Cézanne stripped the genre until only concentration remained, two men bent toward each other like the halves of an arch. He painted five versions, each quieter than the last; in 2011 one of them reportedly became the most expensive painting ever sold, bought for the museums of Qatar.

  • The bottle is the keystone — cover it and the arch collapses
  • No one bets, bluffs or speaks; attention is the whole drama
  • The table tilts — bent to balance the picture, not the cards
