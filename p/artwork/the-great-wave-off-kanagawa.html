The Great Wave off Kanagawa

Katsushika Hokusai · c. 1831 · The Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York · 25.7 × 37.9 cm

The wave rears like a claw; three boats of oarsmen flatten themselves into the troughs; and far away, tiny and eternal, Fuji sits — the only thing in the picture not moving. Printed in the thousands and sold for the price of noodles, made with imported Prussian blue, it became the single most reproduced image in art. The mountain always wins.

  • Fuji is smaller than the foam — that's the whole philosophy
  • The spray dissolves into snowflakes falling toward the mountain
  • This is a print: thousands exist, no two aged alike
