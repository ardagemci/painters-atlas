Primavera

Sandro Botticelli · c. 1480 · Gallerie degli Uffizi, Florence · 202 × 314 cm

Nine figures in an orange grove, five hundred plant species, and not one settled explanation. Mercury stirs the clouds, the Graces dance, Venus presides — and at the right Zephyrus seizes the nymph Chloris, who transforms mid-panel into Flora scattering roses. Painted for a Medici wedding (probably) and read through Neoplatonic philosophy (endlessly), it is the Renaissance's great secular puzzle-picture: mythology given full devotional treatment a century before anyone else dared.

  • Chloris becomes Flora mid-picture — transformation painted as sequence
  • Botanists have identified some five hundred plant species in the meadow
  • Blindfolded Cupid aims at the middle Grace — follow the arrow
