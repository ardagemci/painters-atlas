A Bar at the Folies-Bergère

Édouard Manet · 1882 · The Courtauld Gallery, London · 96 × 130 cm

Manet's last great painting, made while he was dying: a barmaid stands at attention before a mirror that refuses to behave — her reflection slides right, the customer she faces exists only in the glass, the crowd is a glitter of gaslight and opera glasses. Suzon, a real Folies employee, wears the era's saddest expression: perfectly present, entirely elsewhere. Scholars have argued about the mirror for a century; the loneliness has never needed explaining.

  • The reflection is displaced — hers and the customer's; it is not a mistake
  • A trapeze artist's green boots dangle in the upper left corner
  • The signature hides on the label of the rose-wine bottle
