The Kiss

Gustav Klimt · 1907–08 · Österreichische Galerie Belvedere, Vienna · 180 × 180 cm

Two lovers dissolve into one golden shape at the edge of a flowered cliff — his robe patterned in hard black rectangles, hers in soft circles, desire diagrammed in geometry. Klimt, a goldsmith's son, used real gold leaf, making the painting glow like the Byzantine icons it secretly is. Austria bought it straight off the easel, unfinished. Nobody has ever asked for a refund.

  • His pattern is rectangles, hers is circles — geometry as gender
  • Her toes grip the cliff edge; the ecstasy has a precipice
  • The gold is real leaf: the painting emits, not reflects
