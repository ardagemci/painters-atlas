The School of Athens

Raphael · 1509–11 · Vatican Museums, Vatican City · c. 500 × 770 cm (fresco)

Philosophy's family portrait, frescoed for the pope's private library: Plato points up, Aristotle levels his palm, and around them Raphael casts antiquity with modern faces — Leonardo as Plato, Bramante as Euclid, himself watching from the edge, and, added late after he saw the Sistine ceiling next door, Michelangelo brooding on the steps as Heraclitus. A twenty-something painted humanism's entire self-image in one sweep of perspective, and made it look effortless.

  • Plato points to heaven, Aristotle to the world — the whole argument in two hands
  • Michelangelo broods on the steps — added after Raphael saw the ceiling
  • Raphael's own face watches you from the far right edge
