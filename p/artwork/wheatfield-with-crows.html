Wheatfield with Crows

Vincent van Gogh · 1890 · Van Gogh Museum, Amsterdam · 50.5 × 103 cm

Legend calls it his last painting; the letters say otherwise, but the legend won't die because the picture argues for it — a torn double-blue sky, three paths that go nowhere, crows scattering like flung ink. He wrote that these wheatfields expressed 'sadness, extreme loneliness,' and in the same breath that the country was healthy and fortifying. Both are in it, which is the Van Gogh bargain: despair painted with absolute vitality. Weeks later, in these fields, he was shot.

  • Three paths — none of them leads out of the picture
  • The sky is two blues, fighting
  • Each crow is one or two brush-hits: coming or going? Decide
