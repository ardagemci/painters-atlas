Las Meninas

Diego Velázquez · 1656 · Museo del Prado, Madrid · 318 × 276 cm

A princess, her maids, a dwarf, a dog, a painter — and in the mirror at the back, the king and queen standing exactly where you are standing. Velázquez painted the act of being painted, then stepped into it himself, brush raised. Art history's favorite riddle about looking: three and a half centuries in, nobody agrees who this picture is actually of.

  • Find the mirror: the royal couple occupies your spot
  • Velázquez's red cross was added later — legend says by the king
  • The dog is the only one refusing to perform
