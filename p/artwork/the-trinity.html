The Trinity

Andrei Rublev · c. 1425–27 · Tretyakov Gallery, Moscow · 142 × 114 cm

Three angels — Abraham's mysterious visitors, read as the Trinity — sit around a table bearing a single cup, their heads inclined in a conversation conducted entirely in silence. The composition is a circle you feel rather than see; the fourth place at the table faces you. Six centuries of Orthodox theology declared it the icon all icons should imitate. Its calm has outlasted empires.

  • The three staffs are identical: no angel outranks another
  • An invisible circle joins them; the cup is its centre
  • The empty side of the table is your seat
