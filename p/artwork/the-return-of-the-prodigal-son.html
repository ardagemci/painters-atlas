The Return of the Prodigal Son

Rembrandt van Rijn · c. 1661–69 · The State Hermitage Museum, St Petersburg · 262 × 205 cm

Painted in his last years, when he had buried nearly everyone he loved. The son kneels in rags, head shaved like a convict's; the father's hands rest on his shoulders — look closely: one is broad and masculine, the other slender and maternal, both parents in a single embrace. The elder brother stands tall in the shadows at right, hands clasped, doing the arithmetic of fairness. Rembrandt takes no sides; he just makes forgiveness look like light.

  • The father's hands don't match — one fatherly, one motherly
  • The son's feet: one shoe worn through, one fallen off
  • The elder brother at right, withholding what the father gives
