Composition VIII

Wassily Kandinsky · 1923 · Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum, New York · 140 × 201 cm

Ten years after the apocalyptic Composition VII, Kandinsky answered himself: where VII boils, VIII is engineered — circles, wedges and grids on a cool ground, painted in his first Bauhaus year. He now called the circle 'the synthesis of the greatest oppositions,' romantic and geometric at once, and the violet-haloed disc at upper left outranks every form on the canvas. Solomon Guggenheim bought it in 1930, and a museum effectively grew up around the purchase.
