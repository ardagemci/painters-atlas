Sistine Chapel Ceiling

Michelangelo Buonarroti · 1508–12 · Sistine Chapel, Vatican City · c. 40 × 13 m

Michelangelo insisted he was a sculptor, suspected the commission was a rival's trap, fired his assistants within weeks — then painted five thousand square feet of ceiling essentially alone, standing, head thrown back, for four years. Nine scenes from Genesis, seven prophets, five sibyls, twenty ignudi: more than three hundred figures. He wrote a sonnet complaining that his beard pointed at heaven and the brush dripped into his face — 'I am no painter.' The ceiling disagrees.

  • He painted Noah first, God last — watch the figures grow bolder
  • The late panels' figures are twice the size of the early ones
  • Find the ignudi: twenty nudes holding up nothing, magnificently
