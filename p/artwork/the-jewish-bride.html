The Jewish Bride

Rembrandt van Rijn · c. 1665–69 · Rijksmuseum, Amsterdam · 121.5 × 166.5 cm

Nobody knows who they are — the title is a nineteenth-century guess; Rembrandt likely painted a couple as Isaac and Rebecca. What's certain is the tenderness: his hand on her breast, her hand resting on his, a gesture of belonging rather than possession. The golden sleeve is paint troweled on so thick it catches actual light. Van Gogh said he would give ten years of his life to sit before it a fortnight, with only a crust of dry bread to eat.

  • Four hands, each saying something different
  • The sleeve is sculpted paint — palette knife, maybe fingers
  • Neither looks at the other, or at us: both look inward
