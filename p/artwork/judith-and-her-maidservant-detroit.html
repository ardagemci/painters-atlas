Judith and Her Maidservant with the Head of Holofernes

Artemisia Gentileschi · c. 1623–25 · Detroit Institute of Arts, Detroit · 187 × 142 cm

The murder is done; this is the getaway. A single candle lights the tent as Judith, sword still raised, freezes at some sound off-stage — her maid stops mid-motion, the head half-wrapped. Where the Uffizi canvas gives you the deed, Detroit gives you the suspense after it. Artemisia painted candlelight as well as anyone who ever lived, and she knew precisely what a woman listening for footsteps in the dark feels like.

  • Judith's hand shields the candle — the light obeys the plot
  • Both women look off-frame: the danger is where you can't see
  • The head sits half-bagged, handled like laundry — chillingly practical
