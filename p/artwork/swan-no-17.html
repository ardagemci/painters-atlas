The Swan, No. 17

Hilma af Klint · 1915 · Moderna Museet, Stockholm · 150.5 × 151 cm

By the seventeenth painting of the series, the swans are gone: what remains is their collision distilled into a target of concentric color on a field split black and white. Af Klint painted it in 1915 as occult research — opposites uniting, spirit and matter reconciled — and filed it away with the rest of the Temple paintings. Hung today it reads as hard-edge abstraction arriving five decades early. Almost nobody was allowed to see it for seventy years.

  • The black and white halves swap charge across the centre line
