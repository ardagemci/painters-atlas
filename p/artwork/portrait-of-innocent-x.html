Portrait of Innocent X

Diego Velázquez · 1650 · Galleria Doria Pamphilj, Rome · 141 × 119 cm

The most feared portrait ever painted of a pope. Velázquez gave Innocent X everything protocol demanded — the crimson, the lace, the ring — and then told the truth about the face: suspicion, appetite, fatigue, power. The pope reportedly muttered 'troppo vero' — too true — and kept it anyway. It still hangs in the family palace in Rome; Francis Bacon painted dozens of screaming variations without ever daring to stand before the original.
