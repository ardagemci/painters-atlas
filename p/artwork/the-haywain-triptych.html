The Haywain Triptych

Hieronymus Bosch · c. 1512–15 · Museo del Prado, Madrid · 135 × 200 cm (open)

A Flemish proverb said the world is a haystack, and each grabs what he can. Bosch painted the proverb at altarpiece scale: a hay wagon rolling from Eden toward hell, pope and emperor processing behind it, peasants knifing each other for a fistful, lovers on top of the load ignoring everything — and the wagon pulled by half-animal demons nobody thinks to look at. Philip II of Spain, ruler of half the world, kept it at his monastery-palace as a mirror.

  • Everyone holds hay — trace it from pope to pickpocket
  • The wagon's pullers are demons; not one person notices
  • On top of the load: lovers, a praying angel, a demon piping through his nose
