Olympia

Édouard Manet · 1863 · Musée d'Orsay, Paris · 130.5 × 191 cm

Manet repainted Titian's Venus as a working Parisienne who meets your eye and waits. The scandal of 1865 wasn't the nudity — the Salon ran on nudes — it was the gaze, the flat harsh light, the black cat where Titian put a sleeping dog, the servant delivering a client's flowers. Guards were posted to protect the canvas; critics called her a 'female gorilla.' Painting's modern age arguably begins here: the nude stripped of mythology, looking back.

  • Her hand: Titian's Venus yields, Olympia's blocks
  • The choker, the slipper, the orchid — currency, not allegory
  • The black cat arches exactly where the old masters put fidelity
