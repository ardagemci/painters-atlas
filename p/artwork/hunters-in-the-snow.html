The Hunters in the Snow

Pieter Bruegel the Elder · 1565 · Kunsthistorisches Museum, Vienna · 117 × 162 cm

Three tired men and their dogs crest a ridge; below, a whole valley gets on with winter — skaters, chimney fires, crows, a bird trap, and Alps that Bruegel imported into Flanders because the picture needed them. Painted for a rich man's dining room as one month in a cycle of the seasons, it became the West's definitive image of winter: filmmakers from Tarkovsky onward quote it whenever they need cold that feels like memory.

  • Follow the diagonal — dogs, hunters, ridge, skaters, peaks: one long glide
  • The inn fire at left is the only warm color on the panel
  • A single magpie in mid-flight balances the entire composition
