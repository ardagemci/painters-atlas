Lady with an Ermine

Leonardo da Vinci · c. 1489–91 · Czartoryski Museum, Kraków · 54 × 39 cm

Cecilia Gallerani, the Duke of Milan's brilliant seventeen-year-old mistress, turns as if someone just spoke from beyond the frame — and the ermine turns with her, its S-curve repeating hers. The animal is a triple pun: emblem of purity, the duke's own heraldic beast, and a Greek play on her surname. Poets praised the portrait in her lifetime; four and a half centuries later it hangs in Kraków as Poland's most treasured painting, hidden twice from occupying armies.

  • She watches someone we can't see — the picture's real subject
  • The ermine's curve is her curve, restated
  • Her hand may be the best hand in fifteenth-century painting
