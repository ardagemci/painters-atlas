Where Do We Come From? What Are We? Where Are We Going?

Paul Gauguin · 1897–98 · Museum of Fine Arts, Boston · 139 × 375 cm

Painted flat-out in Tahiti after news of his daughter's death, intended — he said — as his testament before a suicide attempt: read it right to left, from the sleeping infant to the old woman awaiting death, with the blue idol of the Beyond watching. Gauguin insisted the title's questions stay unanswered and painted them into the corner like graffiti on a golden wall. Boston got the century's most ambitious painted sermon; Gauguin got seven more ragged years.

  • Read it right to left: infancy, appetite, doubt, the end
  • The title is painted into the top left corner, like graffiti
  • The blue idol watches everything and explains nothing
