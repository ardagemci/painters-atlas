Mont Sainte-Victoire Seen from Bibémus

Paul Cézanne · c. 1897 · Baltimore Museum of Art, Baltimore · 65.1 × 80 cm

Cézanne painted his local mountain more than eighty times, in oil and watercolor, insisting the same motif offered months of study without moving his easel. Here, from the ochre Bibémus quarry where he rented a stone cabin, the peak rises like architecture above rock the color of baked bread. He wasn't recording a view; he was rebuilding nature — 'a harmony parallel to nature,' plane by plane — and teaching the twentieth century how to take painting apart.

  • Sky, mountain and quarry interlock like masonry — count the planes
  • The quarry's ochre repeats inside the mountain's shadows
  • No people, no path, no story: structure is the drama
