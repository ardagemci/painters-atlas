Judith Slaying Holofernes

Artemisia Gentileschi · c. 1620 · Gallerie degli Uffizi, Florence · 199 × 162.5 cm

Most painters gave Judith a delicate shudder. Artemisia gave her leverage. Sleeves rolled, brow set, the widow of Bethulia braces her weight against the general's skull while her maid pins him down — teamwork, not theatre. The blood arcs with hydraulic honesty. Grand dukes reportedly found it too disturbing to hang; modern viewers find it the most alive painting in the room.

  • Judith's forearms carry real anatomical strain — this is labour
  • Blood sprays in arcs, not decorous trickles
  • The maid is young and strong — a co-conspirator, not a witness
