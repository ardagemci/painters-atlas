The Slave Ship

J.M.W. Turner · 1840 · Museum of Fine Arts, Boston · 90.8 × 122.6 cm

Its full title does the accusing: Slavers Throwing Overboard the Dead and Dying — Typhoon Coming On. Turner painted it after reading of the Zong, whose captain had drowned his human cargo for the insurance money. The sea is the true subject — a boiling of gold, blood and rust in which chained hands surface among the fish. Ruskin, who owned it, called it the noblest sea Turner ever painted; in the end he sold it, unable to live with it.

  • Chained hands surface in the right foreground
  • The ship retreats into the typhoon it has earned
  • The sunset and the atrocity share the same reds
