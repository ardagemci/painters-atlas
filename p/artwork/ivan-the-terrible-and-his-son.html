Ivan the Terrible and His Son Ivan

Ilya Repin · 1885 · Tretyakov Gallery, Moscow · 199.5 × 254 cm

Seconds after the blow: Ivan clutches his dying son, eyes blown wide with the understanding that he has just ended his own dynasty. Repin painted history as psychological catastrophe — the brocade, the overturned throne, the carpets, all swallowed by those eyes. Alexander III banned it from exhibition, a first in Russia; a distressed viewer slashed it in 1913, and another attacked it in 2018. It hangs in the Tretyakov behind glass, still radioactive.

  • The Tsar's eyes: horror arriving one heartbeat after the act
  • The son forgives — his hand rests gently on his father's arm
  • An overturned throne in the shadows tells the dynasty's future
