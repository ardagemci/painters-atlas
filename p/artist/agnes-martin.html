Agnes Martin

1912–2004 · Hand-drawn grids, six feet of almost-nothing — 'my paintings are about happiness'

Born on a Saskatchewan farm, Martin swam at Olympic-trial level, taught school, and reached painting slowly via New Mexico and New York's Coenties Slip loft community.

Her six-foot squares carry pencil grids drawn freehand over whisper-pale washes — geometry that trembles, perfection deliberately humanised.
