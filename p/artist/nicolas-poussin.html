Nicolas Poussin

1594–1665 · Reason's painter — antiquity rebuilt one wax figurine at a time

A Norman farmer's son who walked to Paris and then, at thirty, to Rome, Poussin stayed there for the rest of his life — apart from a miserable two-year recall to decorate the Louvre, which he fled.

Poussin composed like a philosopher: stoic shepherds, measured tragedies and landscapes ordered like arguments.
