Marc Chagall

1887–1985 · Fiddlers on roofs, lovers in mid-air — Vitebsk carried around the world

Eldest of nine in a Hasidic family in Vitebsk (now Belarus), Chagall painted his hometown's fiddlers, herring sellers and floating brides for seventy years from Paris, wartime New York and Provence.

Cubism gave him structure, but gravity never applied: in his canvases lovers levitate, cows dream and time runs on memory's clock — Breton claimed him for Surrealism; Chagall politely declined all labels.
