İbrahim Çallı

1882–1960 · The 1914 Generation's ringleader — Turkish light, Paris brushwork

From Çal in Denizli — the hometown became his surname — Çallı reached Istanbul as a penniless clerk's apprentice, won the Paris scholarship, and trained in Fernand Cormon's atelier until the First World War sent the students home.

The returning Paris cohort — 'the 1914 Generation', or simply 'the Çallı Generation' — brought Impressionism to Turkish easels: Bosphorus light, picnics, nudes, dervishes mid-whirl, painted with juicy alla prima conviction.
