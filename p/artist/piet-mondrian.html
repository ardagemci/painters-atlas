Piet Mondrian

1872–1944 · Right angles toward utopia — and a late-life boogie-woogie

A strict Calvinist schoolmaster's son from Amersfoort, Mondrian abstracted himself by degrees: Dutch windmills, theosophy, Cubist Paris, then the grid.

Through De Stijl, founded with Theo van Doesburg in 1917, Mondrian pursued 'neoplasticism': only verticals, horizontals and primaries, balance without symmetry — a visual ethics he believed could redesign the world.
