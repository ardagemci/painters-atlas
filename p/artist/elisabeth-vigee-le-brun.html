Élisabeth Vigée Le Brun

1755–1842 · Marie Antoinette's painter — and Europe's most resourceful exile

A pastellist's daughter who was painting professional portraits in her teens, Vigée Le Brun became Marie Antoinette's favourite painter and confidante.

She produced over 600 portraits with a signature blend of Rococo warmth and Neoclassical clarity, famous for relaxed poses and open-mouthed smiles that scandalised the Salon — teeth were simply not shown.
