Ilya Repin

1844–1930 · Russia's conscience on canvas — barge haulers, Cossacks, a tsar's bloody panic

Repin made realism carry a whole empire: barge haulers in harness, a tsar with his murdered son, Cossacks laughing at a sultan. Painted with virtuoso empathy and a reporter's eye, his pictures became Russia's collective memory — so charged that one has now been attacked twice behind museum glass.

Barge Haulers on the Volga — eleven men dragging a ship like a human harness — became the century's indictment of empire.
