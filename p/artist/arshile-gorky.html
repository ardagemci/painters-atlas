Arshile Gorky

c. 1904–1948 · From Lake Van to Action Painting — the bridge AbEx walked across

Born Vostanik Adoian near Lake Van, Gorky survived the Armenian genocide's death marches; his mother died of starvation in his arms in 1919.

The Artist and His Mother, painted over a decade from a 1912 photograph, turns genocide's memory into one of the century's great double portraits — her hands left unfinished, washed like a fresco's ghost.
