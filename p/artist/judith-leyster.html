Judith Leyster

1609–1660 · The leading star of Haarlem — erased, then found by a monogram

A Haarlem brewer's daughter (the brewery was called the Leister — 'lodestar'), Leyster was among the first women admitted to the painters' Guild of St Luke with her own workshop and apprentices.

Her laughing musicians and candlelit scenes sparkle with Hals-like speed — she sued Hals, in fact, for poaching an apprentice, and won costs.
