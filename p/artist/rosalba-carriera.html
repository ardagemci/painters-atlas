Rosalba Carriera

1673–1757 · The Venetian who made pastel a continental craze

A Venetian lace-maker's daughter, Carriera began by painting snuffbox miniatures on ivory for Grand Tourists, then conquered Europe with pastel portraits — Paris fêted her for a triumphant year in 1720–21, electing her to the royal Academy.

She made pastel — until then a sketching tool — the medium of aristocratic portraiture: powdery skin, shimmering silk, likenesses flattering yet alive, finished in sittings short enough for royal patience.
