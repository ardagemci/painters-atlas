Lucian Freud

1922–2011 · Flesh, scrutinised — thousands of hours per portrait

Grandson of Sigmund Freud, born in Berlin and brought to London in 1933 as the Nazis rose, Freud lived as he painted: on his own terms — gambler, recluse, father of at least fourteen acknowledged children, friend and portraitist of everyone from gangsters to the Queen.

From hypnotic early precision he shifted, with hog-hair brushes and the dry granular pigment cremnitz white, to paint-as-flesh: nudes observed across hundreds of hours until, as he said, the paint 'works as skin does.' Benefits Supervisor Sleeping — Sue Tilley on a battered sofa — sold in 2008 for $33.6 million, then a record for a living artist.
