Nicholas Hilliard

c. 1547–1619 · Elizabeth I in your pocket — portraits the size of a kiss

An Exeter goldsmith's son, Hilliard trained in jewellery before becoming limner — miniature painter — to Queen Elizabeth I, capturing her image for lockets, rings and diplomatic gifts for four decades.

His watercolour-on-vellum miniatures, often no bigger than a playing card, fixed the Elizabethan age's intimate face: ruffs like spun sugar, lovesick young men among symbolic roses, jewels rendered with a goldsmith's truth.
