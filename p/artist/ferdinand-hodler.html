Ferdinand Hodler

1853–1918 · Parallel lives, painted in rhythm

Born into Bern poverty, he buried his entire family — father, mother, all five siblings — mostly to tuberculosis before he reached middle age: 'there was always a corpse in the house,' he said, and death walked through his art accordingly.

He called his method Parallelism: figures repeated in rhythmic symmetry like a ritual frieze, mountains doubled in mirror-still lakes — order as a spiritual argument.
