Piero della Francesca

c. 1415–1492 · Geometry at dawn — the mathematician whose silence outlasted empires

Born in the small Tuscan town of Borgo Sansepolcro, which he never really left in spirit, Piero divided his life between painting and mathematics — his treatises on perspective and the five regular solids circulated among scholars.

His Resurrection — Christ stepping from the tomb over sleeping guards, half the landscape dead and half in leaf — was called 'the best picture in the world' in a famous Aldous Huxley essay.
