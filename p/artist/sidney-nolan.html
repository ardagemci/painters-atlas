Sidney Nolan

1917–1992 · Ned Kelly's black helmet riding an ochre horizon — Australia's myth, manufactured

A Melbourne tram-driver's son, Nolan deserted the army in 1944 and holed up at Heide, the hothouse home of patrons John and Sunday Reed, painting the first Ned Kelly series on their dining table.

His Kelly paintings — the bushranger's armour reduced to a black square with a letterbox slot of sky — gave Australia its defining modern image: part folk hero, part abstract sign, riding flat Ripolin-enamel deserts.
