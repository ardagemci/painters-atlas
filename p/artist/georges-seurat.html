Georges Seurat

1859–1891 · Two years, three million dots — Sunday afternoon, frozen forever

A quiet, methodical Parisian of independent means — colleagues called him 'the notary' for his composure — Seurat kept his common-law wife Madeleine Knobloch and their infant son secret even from his mother until days before his death.

Armed with the colour science of Chevreul and Rood, Seurat replaced Impressionist instinct with system: pure pigment in tiny dots, mixed by the eye at distance.
