Matthias Grünewald

c. 1470–1528 · The Isenheim Altarpiece — agony and radiance for a plague hospital

His real name was probably Mathis Gothardt; 'Grünewald' was a biographer's error that stuck.

For the Antonite hospital at Isenheim, which treated victims of ergot poisoning, he painted Christianity's most harrowing Crucifixion — Christ's skin studded with sores like the patients viewing it — which opens, wing by wing, into the most incandescent Resurrection in art.
