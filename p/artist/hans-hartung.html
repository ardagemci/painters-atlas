Hans Hartung

1904–1989 · Lightning, archived — gesture at the speed of a nervous system

A Leipzig doctor's son who as a boy filled 'lightning notebooks', sketching storms before the thunder arrived, Hartung fled Nazi Germany, joined the French Foreign Legion under a false name, and lost a leg at Belfort in 1944.

His black calligraphic bundles on luminous grounds — catalogued with cool T-numbers, 'T' for toile — made him Art Informel's defining stroke: spontaneity, he confessed late, often rehearsed in small studies then enlarged with a craftsman's calm, a scandal of honesty his admirers forgave.
