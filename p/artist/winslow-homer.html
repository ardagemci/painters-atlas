Winslow Homer

1836–1910 · The sea, without sentiment — America's weather-beaten realist

A Boston bookseller's son, Homer covered the Civil War as an illustrator for Harper's Weekly, taught himself oils, and spent twenty months in an English fishing village watching women haul nets against the North Sea — a turn that hardened his art.

His war scenes skipped the glory for boredom and aftermath; his post-war children and farm girls carry an elegy inside the sunlight.
