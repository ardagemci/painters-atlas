Theophanes the Greek

c. 1340–c. 1410 · Byzantium's lightning, loosed on Russian walls — Rublev's teacher

A master from Constantinople who had frescoed, by a contemporary's count, some forty churches across Byzantium and the Black Sea, Theophanes arrived in Novgorod around 1370 and spent his last decades electrifying Russian art.

His Novgorod frescoes are unlike anything before them: saints in near-monochrome ochre, modelled by slashes of white — 'lightning strokes' — as if holiness were voltage.
