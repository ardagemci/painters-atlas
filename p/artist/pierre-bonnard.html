Pierre Bonnard

1867–1947 · Marthe in the bath, forever — colour as slow memory

A reluctant law graduate turned 'very Japanese Nabi', Bonnard spent his life painting one woman: Marthe de Méligny, his model, companion and finally wife, whose long baths and fragile health structured their reclusive days in Normandy and Le Cannet.

He painted from memory, never the motif — canvases tacked unstretched to the wall, colour adjusted for years until rooms glowed like recollection itself: tablecloths hotter than the Midi, Marthe ageless in violet bathwater.
