William Hogarth

1697–1764 · Satire in series — the moralist with the engraver's burin

Son of a Latin teacher who spent years in debtors' prison — a wound Hogarth never forgot — he apprenticed as a silver engraver and clawed his way up London's image trade.

Hogarth invented the picture-story: 'modern moral subjects' like A Rake's Progress and Marriage A-la-Mode, painted, then engraved, then sold by subscription to a mass public — the ancestor of the comic strip and the editorial cartoon.
