Pieter Bruegel the Elder

c. 1525–1569 · Peasant weddings, proverb worlds and the first great snow in art

Bruegel invented the view from everywhere: whole valleys, whole villages, whole seasons on one panel, with humanity seen from just high enough to love and laugh at simultaneously. Skip his peasants at your peril — the wedding feasts and snowbound hunters carry more philosophy than most allegories.

In barely forty surviving panels Bruegel built a complete world: seasons turning over village life, children's games catalogued by the hundred, proverbs literalised into absurdist theatre, and Babel rising like a doomed termite mound.
