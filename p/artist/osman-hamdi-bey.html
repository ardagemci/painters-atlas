Osman Hamdi Bey

1842–1910 · The Tortoise Trainer — Orientalism, corrected from the inside

Son of a grand vizier, trained in Paris law and in the studios of Gérôme and Boulanger, Osman Hamdi returned to run half of Ottoman culture: founder-director of the Imperial Museum, founder of the fine-arts academy, author of the empire's first antiquities laws, and excavator of Sidon, where his team raised the Alexander Sarcophagus.

He used Orientalism's own polished technique to overturn its clichés: instead of harem fantasy, his figures read books, debate theology, train tortoises by candlelight — Ottomans with inner lives, painted by one.
