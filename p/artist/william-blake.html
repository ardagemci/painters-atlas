William Blake

1757–1827 · Visions printed at home — heaven and hell, hand-coloured

A London hosier's son who saw a tree filled with angels at eight and never stopped seeing, Blake trained as an engraver and lived poor, radical and unwavering.

Inventing 'illuminated printing' — relief-etched plates combining his poems and images, hand-coloured copy by copy — he created the Songs of Innocence and of Experience, The Marriage of Heaven and Hell, and prophetic epics nobody bought.
