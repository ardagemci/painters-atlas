Édouard Manet

1832–1883 · The reluctant revolutionary in a top hat

Manet is the hinge: the last old master and the first modern painter on one easel. He took Titian and Velázquez, stripped the varnish of myth, and painted his own Paris looking back at the viewer — scandalizing the Salon so thoroughly that modern art's origin story is usually dated to his Olympia.

The Déjeuner — a nude woman picnicking with dressed dandies — detonated the 1863 Salon des Refusés; Olympia, a courtesan returning the viewer's stare, required guards at the next Salon.
