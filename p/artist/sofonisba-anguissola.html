Sofonisba Anguissola

c. 1532–1625 · The noblewoman who proved, at the Spanish court, what a woman's brush could do

Born to minor nobility in Cremona, Sofonisba was one of six sisters educated like Renaissance princes — and the first woman painter to win international fame.

Barred as a woman from studying anatomy or large religious commissions, she conquered the genre open to her: the portrait, charged with wit and inner life.
