Stanisław Wyspiański

1869–1907 · Playwright, stained-glass visionary, pastel portraitist — Young Poland in one man

Matejko's most gifted pupil — and gentlest rebel — Wyspiański remade every art Kraków offered: painting, stained glass, furniture, typography, and the national drama (his play The Wedding remains Poland's most performed).

His Franciscan church windows — God the Father erupting from cosmic chaos — and the floral wall-paintings beneath them are Art Nouveau at cathedral scale and Slavic pitch.
