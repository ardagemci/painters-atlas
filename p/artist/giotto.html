Giotto di Bondone

c. 1267–1337 · The shepherd boy who taught painting to weep

Vasari's legend has Cimabue discovering a Tuscan shepherd boy drawing sheep on a rock with a pointed stone; documented fact has Giotto running Italy's busiest workshop, buying farms, lending money and ending as chief architect of Florence Cathedral, whose bell tower still bears his name.

In Padua's Scrovegni Chapel he repainted the rules: figures with weight and backs, grief that bends bodies, a kiss between Joachim and Anna that is the first believable embrace in European art.
