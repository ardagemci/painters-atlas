Kerry James Marshall

b. 1955 · Mastry — painting Black life into the canon, in unapologetic black

Born in Birmingham, Alabama at the civil-rights flashpoint and raised near Watts in Los Angeles — 'You can't grow up there and not feel like you've got some kind of social responsibility' — Marshall studied at Otis, settled in Chicago, and taught for years while building his project with deliberate patience.

Confronting the near-absence of Black figures in museum painting, he resolved to fill the gap genre by genre — garden scenes, barbershops, beauty salons, housing-project pastorals — with figures painted in literal, luminous carbon black.
