Katsushika Hokusai

1760–1849 · Old Man Mad About Painting — and one immortal wave

Hokusai gave the world its most reproduced image and Japan its restless genius — thirty name changes, ninety addresses, 'mad about painting' to the last. The Great Wave taught the West that flatness, cropping and pattern could carry monumental force. Monet and Van Gogh collected him like scripture.

His Thirty-six Views of Mount Fuji, produced in his seventies, gave the world the Great Wave: claw-crested water dwarfing boats while Fuji sits tiny and eternal — printed in the new imported Prussian blue.
