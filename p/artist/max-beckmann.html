Max Beckmann

1884–1950 · Triptychs for a century in flames — the self-portraitist in the tuxedo

A Leipzig merchant's son who measured himself against Rembrandt early, Beckmann broke down as a WWI medical orderly and rebuilt his art from the rubble.

He refused both abstraction and -isms: 'I paint the visible world plus the inner experience.' The Night's torture chamber announced Weimar's nerves; the nine great triptychs, beginning with Departure, staged exile, cabaret and myth like medieval altars rebuilt for the age of passports.
