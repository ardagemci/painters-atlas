Henri Rousseau

1844–1910 · Le Douanier — jungles dreamed by a man who never left France

A toll collector at the Paris customs gates — hence 'Le Douanier' — Rousseau took up painting seriously around forty, retired on a tiny pension at forty-nine, and lived hand-to-mouth giving violin lessons.

Critics laughed at his stiff perspective and solemn lions for twenty years of independent Salons; he serenely quoted the mockery as praise.
