Raja Ravi Varma

1848–1906 · The gods in oil paint — and on every calendar in India

Born into the Travancore royal house in Kerala, Ravi Varma taught himself European oil technique from palace paintings and visiting portraitists, then conquered colonial India's exhibitions — a Vienna medal in 1873 announced him internationally.

He gave Hindu epics the full apparatus of academic oil painting — Shakuntala glancing back under the pretence of a thorn, Saraswati and Lakshmi with human warmth — and then, decisively, founded a Bombay lithographic press so the gods could hang in every home.
