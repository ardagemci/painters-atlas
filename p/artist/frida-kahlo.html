Frida Kahlo

1907–1954 · 'I paint my own reality' — the unflinching self, garlanded in thorns

Kahlo painted her own reality — a body broken by a bus, a heart broken by Rivera — in the deadpan style of votive miracles. A third of her work is self-portrait, none of it self-pity. She turned autobiography into iconography, and the world is still wearing it.

Of her roughly 150 paintings, a third are self-portraits: monkey-attended, thorn-necklaced, twinned, split open — autobiography conducted in votive-painting style with surgical honesty.
