Odilon Redon

1840–1916 · Floating eyes, smiling spiders — then, at fifty, an explosion of flowers

A sickly Bordeaux child raised lonely on a remote estate, Redon studied botany with the naturalist Clavaud and drawing in darkness of his own temperament.

His 'noirs' — charcoal swamps where eyes balloon skyward and spiders smile — illustrated Poe and Flaubert and gave the Symbolists their dream-grammar.
