Chaïm Soutine

1893–1943 · Paint that trembles like meat

Tenth of eleven children in a Jewish shtetl near Minsk, beaten as a boy for the sin of drawing, he clawed his way through Vilnius art school to Paris's La Ruche — the beehive of penniless immigrant painters — where Modigliani became his closest friend, protector and portraitist.

Soutine painted as if the world were perishable: hanging beef that he kept fresh with buckets of blood while neighbors summoned the police, portraits of pastry cooks and bellboys twisted like flames, Céret landscapes that heave like seasickness.
