Correggio

1489–1534 · Ceilings that open to heaven — the Baroque, a century early

Antonio Allegri, called Correggio after his small hometown near Parma, lived a quiet provincial life that biographers found maddeningly undramatic — marriage, children, steady work — and died suddenly at about forty-five.

His Parma Cathedral dome dissolves architecture into a vortex of ascending bodies — the first true 'ceiling of heaven', ancestor of every Baroque illusion.
