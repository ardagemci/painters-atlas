Emily Kame Kngwarreye

c. 1910–1996 · Eight years, three thousand paintings — a whole country's Dreaming, late and luminous

An Anmatyerre elder from Utopia, north-east of Alice Springs, Kngwarreye worked stock camps and ceremony grounds for seven decades before touching acrylic paint around age seventy-eight, after a decade of community batik.

Her surfaces evolved at decade-speed: dotted fields like seed-scatter, then raking parallel 'yam lines', then the bare, slabbed colour of the last years — each phase anchored in the yam, the emu, the ceremonial body-paint of Awelye.
