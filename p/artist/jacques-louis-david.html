Jacques-Louis David

1748–1825 · Painting as politics — Rome's virtues, the Revolution's martyrs, Napoleon's crown

A Paris silk merchant's son scarred in a fencing accident — the tumour slurred his speech for life — David rose through the Academy he would later help abolish.

The Oath of the Horatii hit the 1785 Salon like a drumbeat: rigid Roman resolve scrubbed clean of Rococo sugar, an aesthetic revolution four years ahead of the political one.
