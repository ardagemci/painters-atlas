Gustave Caillebotte

1848–1894 · Paris in the rain — the Impressionist who bankrolled Impressionism

Heir to a textile fortune, Caillebotte painted, collected, sailed, gardened and underwrote the Impressionist exhibitions — often paying the rent and buying the unsold canvases.

The Floor Scrapers — shirtless workmen planing a parquet against the light — was rejected by the Salon for 'vulgar subject matter'; Paris Street; Rainy Day mapped the new Haussmann city with photographic plunge and umbrella-grey poise.
