Isaac Levitan

1860–1900 · The mood landscape — Russia's melancholy, painted at dusk

Born poor and Jewish in a Lithuanian shtetl, Levitan was twice expelled from Moscow during anti-Jewish edicts even as the city celebrated him.

He invented what Russians call the 'mood landscape': nothing happens — a river bend, a darkening sky, the road convicts walked to Siberia — and everything is felt.
