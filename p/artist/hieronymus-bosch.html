Hieronymus Bosch

c. 1450–1516 · Hell's most inventive travel agent, five centuries early

Five centuries before Surrealism had a manifesto, Bosch painted moral philosophy as hallucination — tree-bodied demons, egg-shaped hells, humanity swarming toward its appetites. Nobody knows quite what he meant, which is the engine of his immortality: every age reads its own nightmare into him.

Across roughly twenty-five surviving works Bosch painted morality as hallucination: paradise sliding into orgy sliding into a hell of musical instruments turned torture devices.
