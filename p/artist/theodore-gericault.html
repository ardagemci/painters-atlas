Théodore Géricault

1791–1824 · The Raft of the Medusa — journalism at the scale of history painting

Born rich in Rouen, Géricault lived like one of his runaway horses: a scandalous affair with his young aunt-by-marriage, a hidden son, England, racetracks, and a string of riding accidents that — with tuberculosis of the spine — killed him at thirty-two.

For the Raft he interviewed survivors of the Medusa shipwreck, built a model raft, and studied severed limbs from the morgue in his studio — modern investigative method fused with Michelangelo's bodies.
