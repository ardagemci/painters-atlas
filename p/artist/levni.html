Abdulcelil Levni

c. 1680–1732 · The Tulip Era's last great miniaturist — colour was literally his name

Born in Edirne, Abdulcelil Çelebi rose through the palace workshop to become chief painter under Ahmed III, taking the pen-name 'Levnî' — 'the colourful'.

His masterwork illustrates the 1720 festival for the sultan's sons: fireworks over the Golden Horn, dancing, oiled wrestlers, sugar gardens floated on barges — 137 paintings looser, wittier and more atmospheric than anything before.
