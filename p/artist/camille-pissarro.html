Camille Pissarro

1830–1903 · The patriarch — the only painter in all eight Impressionist exhibitions

Born on St Thomas in the Danish West Indies to a French-Jewish merchant family, Pissarro chose painting over the family business and Paris over the tropics.

The only artist to show in all eight Impressionist exhibitions, he was mentor to an improbable span: Cézanne ('humble and colossal Pissarro') worked beside him at Pontoise, Gauguin called him master, and at fifty-five he humbly adopted the pointillism of men half his age.
