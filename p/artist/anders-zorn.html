Anders Zorn

1860–1920 · Three strokes, and the water moves

Born to an unmarried brewery worker in Mora and raised on his grandparents' farm, he never met his German father — and ended life as Sweden's international celebrity painter, wealthy enough to collect a museum's worth of art around his Mora estate.

Zorn's brush was famous for economy: water, skin and lamplight conjured in strokes you can count.
