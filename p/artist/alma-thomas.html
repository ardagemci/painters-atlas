Alma Thomas

1891–1978 · Mosaic gardens at 75 — the retired teacher who joined the space age

Born in Columbus, Georgia, Thomas moved with her family to Washington DC to escape Jim Crow's worst, became Howard University's first fine-arts graduate, and taught junior-high art for thirty-five years.

Her signature arrived with the late start: irregular tiles of pure colour marching in stripes and circles — azalea gardens seen through wind, Earth seen from orbit (the Apollo missions thrilled her into a 'space series').
