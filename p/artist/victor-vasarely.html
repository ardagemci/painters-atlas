Victor Vasarely

1906–1997 · The grandfather of Op — grids that breathe, art for everyone

Born in Pécs, Hungary, Vasarely abandoned medical studies for Budapest's 'Mühely', the local Bauhaus, then built a Paris career in advertising graphics whose optical tricks ripened, by the 1950s, into a complete art.

Zebra (1937) — two beasts woven from warping stripes — is op art's commonly cited big bang; the Vega series bulged checkerboard grids into spheres that swell off the wall.
