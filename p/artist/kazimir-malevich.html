Kazimir Malevich

1879–1935 · The Black Square — painting's full stop and new alphabet

Born near Kyiv to a Polish family, Malevich rose through icon-influenced peasant scenes and Cubo-Futurism to revolutionary Petrograd's avant-garde summit.

At the 1915 '0,10' exhibition he hung Black Square across the room's corner — the place of the icon — declaring painting reduced 'to the zero of form' and rebuilt from pure feeling: Suprematism.
