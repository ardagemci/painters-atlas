George Stubbs

1724–1806 · Whistlejacket, life-size — anatomy raised to majesty

A Liverpool currier's son, self-taught and stubborn, Stubbs spent eighteen months in a remote Lincolnshire farmhouse dissecting horse carcasses suspended from the ceiling, drawing every layer — then etched the plates himself when no engraver would.

Whistlejacket — a rearing Arabian, life-size, against bare gold ground, no rider, no landscape, no master — is the most radical portrait of the British eighteenth century, and it's of a horse.
