Antoine Watteau

1684–1721 · The melancholy inventor of the endless garden party

Born in Valenciennes, a Flemish town only recently French, Watteau arrived in Paris poor, intense and already tubercular.

The Academy had to invent a category for his reception piece: the fête galante — silk-clad lovers drifting through parks where music is implied and dusk is always near.
