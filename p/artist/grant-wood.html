Grant Wood

1891–1942 · American Gothic — the most parodied painting on Earth

An Iowa farm boy who lost his stern Quaker father at ten, Wood trained as a metalworker, taught school, and made four trips to Europe that ended, paradoxically, in conversion to home: 'I had to go to France to appreciate Iowa.' He led the Regionalist movement from Cedar Rapids and Iowa City until cancer killed him the night before his fifty-first birthday.

American Gothic — his sister Nan and his dentist posed before a carpenter-Gothic cottage window — became the national mirror: read as satire in Depression-era cities, as homage on the farms, and as both forever after.
