Leonora Carrington

1917–2011 · The runaway debutante who out-dreamed the Surrealists

Born to a textile-fortune family in Lancashire and expelled from two convent schools, Carrington fled her debutante season for Max Ernst at twenty.

Her paintings are fairy tales with teeth: white horses, hyenas at the table, alchemical kitchens where giantesses shelter geese — myth cooked from Celtic nursery lore, the Kabbalah and Mexican magic.
