Valentin Serov

1865–1911 · Portraits the sitters feared

Son of a famous composer, he was handed to Repin as a boy and trained at the master's kitchen table; at twenty-two he painted Girl with Peaches — Vera Mamontova in a sunlit dining room — and Russian painting stepped into modern light in one sitting.

Serov's portraits flattered no one and fascinated everyone — 'I am not a photographer,' he warned sitters, and the results could end friendships.
