Rogier van der Weyden

c. 1399–1464 · The painter of tears — emotion engineered with Flemish precision

Born in Tournai and trained under Robert Campin, Rogier became official painter to the city of Brussels — a post created for him — and the most influential Northern artist of his century, with patrons from the Burgundian court to the Medici and the kings of Castile.

His Descent from the Cross compresses ten grieving figures into a shallow golden box, Mary's swoon rhyming with her son's dead arc — choreography of pure sorrow that painters copied for a century.
