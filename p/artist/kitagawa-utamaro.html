Kitagawa Utamaro

c. 1753–1806 · The floating world's great portraitist of women

Almost nothing is certain about his origins — birthplace disputed, family unknown — until he surfaces in Edo as a pupil of Toriyama Sekien and, decisively, as the house artist of the brilliant publisher Tsutaya Jūzaburō, above whose shop he lived.

Utamaro turned the courtesans, waitresses and teahouse beauties of Edo into the most refined portraiture of the floating world — inventing the ōkubi-e 'large head' close-up, setting faces against shimmering mica grounds, and finding individual psychology inside an idealized type.
