Joshua Reynolds

1723–1792 · The Grand Manner, by committee of one — first president of the Royal Academy

A Devon schoolmaster's son, Reynolds studied Italy's masters, then organised British art into existence: founding president of the Royal Academy (1768), knighted, host of London's great dinner table — Johnson, Burke, Garrick, the original 'Club'.

His 'Grand Manner' dressed English sitters in the poses of gods and Caesars — actresses as muses, colonels as Apollo — flattery elevated to theory in his fifteen Discourses, the most influential art lectures in English.
