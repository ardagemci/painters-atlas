Otto Dix

1891–1969 · Weimar's cold mirror — the war painted by a machine-gunner who survived it

A Gera foundry worker's son, Dix volunteered in 1914 and served as a machine-gunner at the Somme — nightmares of crawling through ruined houses pursued him for decades.

His Der Krieg etchings — gas, craters, the dead in wire — stand with Goya's Disasters as war reporting from inside the nightmare; the War triptych reframes it as a Grünewald altarpiece.
