Vilhelm Hammershøi

1864–1916 · Grey rooms, a woman's turned back — silence you can hang on a wall

A Copenhagen merchant's son, Hammershøi lived quietly with his wife Ida, painting their successive apartments — above all Strandgade 30 — in greys, whites and the dust of northern light.

He painted perhaps sixty views of his own rooms: doors ajar onto further doors, Ida from behind reading or simply standing, sunlight crossing a floor as the day's whole event.
