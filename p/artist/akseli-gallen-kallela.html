Akseli Gallen-Kallela

1865–1931 · The Kalevala given a face — painting a nation before it existed

Born Axél Gallén in Pori — he Finnicised his Swedish name in 1907, a manifesto in itself — Gallen-Kallela studied in Paris but found his subject in Finland's wilderness and its folk epic, the Kalevala.

His Kalevala paintings — the Sampo defended in a storm of swords, a mother gathering her son's body from death's black river — fused Symbolism, Art Nouveau flatness and folk ornament into images Finland adopted as its self-portrait.
