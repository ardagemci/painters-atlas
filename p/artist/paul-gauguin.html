Paul Gauguin

1848–1903 · The stockbroker who fled the world — colour as myth, paradise as problem

Gauguin quit the stock exchange, then Impressionism, then Europe itself, insisting painting should serve feeling and symbol rather than sight. His flat vermilions and invented paradises rewired modern color — and came at a real human cost in Polynesia that the paintings both conceal and record.

At Pont-Aven he invented Synthetism: flat planes of unmixed colour bounded by dark line — a red field for a wrestling vision, because feeling outranks fact.
