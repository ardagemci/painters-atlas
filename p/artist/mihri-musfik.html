Mihri Müşfik Hanım

1885–1954 · The first lady of Turkish painting — from the palace to a potter's field

Born into an aristocratic Istanbul household — her father a pasha and professor of medicine — Mihri Rasim defied convention serially: studying art in Rome and Paris, marrying and divorcing on her own terms, and becoming the Ottoman Empire's first prominent woman painter.

As director of drawing at the new Women's Fine Arts School in Istanbul (1914), she fought through scandalous innovations — live models for female students among them — and trained the republic's first generation of women artists.
