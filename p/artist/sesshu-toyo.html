Sesshū Tōyō

1420–1506 · Splashed-ink mountains — Zen's greatest brush

A samurai family's son sent young to a Zen temple, Sesshū trained in Kyoto before sailing with a trade mission to Ming China in 1467 — studying the masters at the source and finding them, he reported with monkish cheek, less instructive than China's actual mountains.

His Long Scroll unrolls sixteen metres of seasons, cliffs and travellers in strokes like axe cuts — the summit of Japanese ink landscape.
