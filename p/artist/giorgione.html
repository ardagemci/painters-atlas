Giorgione

c. 1477–1510 · Half a dozen paintings, five centuries of mystery

Of Giorgio da Castelfranco — 'Giorgione', big George — we know almost nothing certain: born in the Veneto, trained beside Titian under Bellini, famously handsome, a lute player beloved at Venetian gatherings.

Perhaps six paintings are securely his, and they changed everything: The Tempest — a soldier, a nursing woman, a lightning bolt, and no agreed meaning — may be Western art's first painting of pure mood.
