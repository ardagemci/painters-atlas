Fernando Botero

1932–2023 · Boterismo — volume as joy, satire and national monument

Born in Medellín and briefly enrolled in matador school before choosing the gentler bullring of art, Botero left for Europe at twenty to copy the old masters and never stopped conversing with them.

His inflated figures — generals, cardinals, dancers, plump Mona Lisas — began, he said, with a mandolin whose soundhole he drew too small in 1956: suddenly volume itself was the style, 'Boterismo.' Dismissed by some critics as decorative, he answered with the searing Abu Ghraib series (2005), donated to Berkeley, proving the rounded style could carry rage as well as tenderness.
