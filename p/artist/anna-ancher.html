Anna Ancher

1859–1935 · Sunlight on a blue wall, forever

The only member of the Skagen colony actually born in Skagen — daughter of the Brøndums Inn where the painters gathered — she grew up serving the artists dinner and out-painted most of them.

While the colony's men chased fishermen and heroics, Anna Ancher painted what was actually there: sunlight crossing a blue wall, women plucking geese, a funeral's black clothes against white light.
