Ivan Aivazovsky

1817–1900 · Six thousand seas, painted entirely from memory

Born Hovhannes Aivazian to a poor Armenian family in Crimean Feodosia, Aivazovsky rose through the St Petersburg Academy to become official painter of the Russian Navy and Europe's most celebrated marine artist.

He painted some 6,000 canvases — nearly all the sea, nearly all from memory: 'the movement of living elements cannot be captured from nature.' The Ninth Wave, survivors clinging to a mast under a sun that might be salvation, is Russian Romanticism's signature image.
