René Magritte

1898–1967 · This is not a painter — the bowler-hatted saboteur of common sense

Born in provincial Belgium, Magritte was thirteen when his mother drowned herself in the Sambre — she was found, the story goes, with her nightdress over her face, an image readers find everywhere in his veiled heads.

Magritte painted impossibilities with a notary's neatness: a pipe captioned 'this is not a pipe,' day and night sharing one street, a green apple obscuring a bowler-hatted everyman, men raining on the suburbs.
