James Ensor

1860–1949 · Masks, skeletons, and Christ stuck in traffic

Born in Ostend above his family's souvenir shop — carnival masks, seashells, chinoiserie — and he essentially never left: eighty-nine years in one seaside town, most of them in rooms above the masks that colonized his art.

He co-founded the Brussels avant-garde group Les XX — which then rejected his masterpiece: Christ's Entry into Brussels in 1889, a four-metre carnival mob swallowing a tiny Christ, stayed rolled in his attic studio for decades.
