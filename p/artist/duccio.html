Duccio di Buoninsegna

c. 1255–1319 · The Maestà carried through Siena to the sound of bells

Siena's greatest painter appears in the archives mostly through fines — for debts, for declining militia duty, for what one scholar calls 'a distaste for civic obligation matched only by his genius.' Whatever his citizenship record, when his Maestà was finished in 1311 the city closed its shops and carried the altarpiece from studio to cathedral in procession, trumpets and bells sounding.

The Maestà — Madonna enthroned among ranked saints on gold, backed by dozens of narrative panels — is the summit of Sienese painting: Byzantine splendour warmed into swaying line and human glance.
