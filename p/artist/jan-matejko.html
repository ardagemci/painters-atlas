Jan Matejko

1838–1893 · History painting as national life-support

Born in Kraków when Poland existed only in memory — partitioned off every map — Matejko devoted his life to painting the nation back into being.

The Battle of Grunwald — four by ten metres of colliding knights — and Prussian Homage staged Poland's triumphs at overwhelming scale; Stańczyk, the court jester sitting grief-struck while the ball glitters behind him, gave the nation its conscience in a single figure.
