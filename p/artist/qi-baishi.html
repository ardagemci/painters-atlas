Qi Baishi

1864–1957 · Shrimp in ink — a carpenter who became China's most beloved brush

Born to a poor farming family in Hunan, Qi Baishi worked as a carpenter until woodcarving led him to painting manuals and a late, self-taught start.

His shrimp — translucent bodies in three tones of ink, antennae alive with current — took decades of refinement and a courtyard tank of live models; crabs, gourds, frogs and wisteria received the same devotion.
