Barnett Newman

1905–1970 · The zip — one vertical line, carrying the sublime

A Bronx-born son of Polish-Jewish immigrants, Newman wrote criticism, studied ornithology, taught substitute art classes — and once ran a write-in campaign for mayor of New York on a platform of clean air, playgrounds and a municipal opera.

Onement I — a cadmium 'zip' splitting a maroon field — became his lifelong instrument: vast colour walls (Vir Heroicus Sublimis runs five and a half metres) interrupted by vertical pulses meant to make the viewer feel, he said, their own scale and presence.
