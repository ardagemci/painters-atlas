Diego Velázquez

1599–1660 · The painter's painter — truth at court, mirrors within mirrors

Velázquez painted what was actually there — air included. Popes muttered 'too true'; Impressionists called him the painter of painters. Las Meninas turned looking itself into the subject, and his feathery late strokes, meaningless up close and alive at distance, taught Manet everything.

From bodegón kitchen scenes to popes and kings, Velázquez painted what was actually there — air included.
