Andy Warhol

1928–1987 · The Factory's silver mirror — fifteen minutes, infinitely reprinted

Warhol pointed painting's eye at the supermarket and the front page — soup cans, Marilyns, car crashes — and silkscreened them until repetition itself became the message. 'I want to be a machine' was the century's slyest artistic confession. Image culture still runs on his operating system.

In 1962 he exhibited thirty-two Campbell's soup cans and erased the line between supermarket and museum.
