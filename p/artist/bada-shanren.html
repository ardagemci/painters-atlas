Bada Shanren

c. 1626–1705 · Fish with rolling eyes — a fallen prince's magnificent sulk

Born Zhu Da, a prince of the Ming imperial house, he watched his dynasty fall to the Manchus in 1644 and vanished into a Buddhist monastery.

His ink creatures are unforgettable: fish and birds with eyes rolled upward in cosmic disdain, one-legged birds balancing like questions, lotus stems slashed in seconds.
