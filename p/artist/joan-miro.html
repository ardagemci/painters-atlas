Joan Miró

1893–1983 · Constellations and ladders of escape — the assassin of painting, smiling

A Barcelona goldsmith's son who suffered a breakdown clerking in an office, Miró split his life between Paris's avant-garde and the Catalan farm at Mont-roig that anchored everything.

The Farm — every leaf and lizard of his family's land inventoried — was bought by Hemingway, who said it held everything Spain made you feel.
