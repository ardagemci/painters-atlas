Georges de La Tour

1593–1652 · One candle against the dark

A baker's son from Lorraine who married into minor nobility, La Tour grew wealthy and reportedly unpopular in plague- and war-ravaged Lunéville, where archives record grain dealing and sharp lawsuits alongside his royal title of 'Painter to the King.' He died in an epidemic in 1652 — and was then utterly forgotten for over 250 years.

La Tour reduced Caravaggism to its essence: a single candle, a circle of faces, geometry smoothed almost to abstraction.
