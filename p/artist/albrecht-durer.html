Albrecht Dürer

1471–1528 · The Renaissance arrives in the North — by printing press

Dürer made the print an art form and himself the first international art brand — the AD monogram was famous from Lisbon to Kraków while he was still in his twenties. He painted himself like Christ, drew weeds and hares like scripture, and put German art permanently on the map.

Dürer made the print a major art form: his woodcuts and engravings — the Apocalypse, Melencolia I, the Rhinoceros — circulated through Europe like wildfire and made him the first artist with an international public in his own lifetime.
