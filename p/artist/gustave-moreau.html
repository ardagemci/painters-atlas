Gustave Moreau

1826–1898 · Salomé in jewels — the hermit who taught the Fauves

A Paris architect's son of private means, Moreau lived almost monastically with his mother in a house he slowly converted into his own museum.

His Salomé pictures — the dancer confronted by the Baptist's floating, blood-haloed head — distilled Symbolism before the word: myth re-dreamed as jewelled hallucination, every surface encrusted like a reliquary.
