Wifredo Lam

1902–1982 · The Jungle — Afro-Cuba reclaims modernism's masks

Born in Sagua la Grande to a Chinese immigrant father — eighty-four at his birth — and a mother of African and Spanish descent, with a godmother who was a Santería priestess, Lam carried three continents in his name.

The Jungle — bamboo-limbed figures with crescent faces rising from sugarcane — hangs near MoMA's entrance as modernism's great reply to itself: Picasso had borrowed African masks; Lam took them home.
