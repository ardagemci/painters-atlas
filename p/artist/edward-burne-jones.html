Edward Burne-Jones

1833–1898 · Knights, dreams and stairways to nowhere

A Birmingham frame-maker's son bound for the church until, at Oxford, he met William Morris and the two discovered Rossetti — theology lost, art history gained.

The second Pre-Raphaelite wave was largely his: medieval dreams slowed to a trance, androgynous figures descending stairs that lead nowhere, Arthurian legend painted as though remembered from another life.
