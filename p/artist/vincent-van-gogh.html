Vincent van Gogh

1853–1890 · Ten years, nine hundred paintings, one sale — then the whole world

Ten years, nine hundred paintings, one sale — then the whole world. Van Gogh turned brushwork into handwriting and color into confession; every stroke is signed by a nervous system. He is proof that painting can carry a life's entire voltage, and modern art's most loved cautionary saint.

From the earth-dark Potato Eaters he vaulted, via Paris and Japanese prints, into colour as pure feeling: sunflowers like suns, a night sky churning over Saint-Rémy painted from his asylum window, cypresses twisting like green flames.
