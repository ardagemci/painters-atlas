Romare Bearden

1911–1988 · Harlem, cut and pasted — jazz you can frame

Born in Charlotte, North Carolina, and raised in Harlem — his mother's salon hosted Hughes and Ellington — Bearden balanced painting with a full-time New York social-work caseload for nearly three decades.

His Projections and the works after — sliced photographs, painted paper, fabric — reassembled Black Southern memory and Harlem streets into stained-glass jazz: conjur women, trains, baptisms, Saturday kitchens.
