Bartolomé Esteban Murillo

1617–1682 · Street urchins and floating Madonnas — Seville's gentle Baroque

Orphaned at ten, youngest of fourteen children of a Seville barber-surgeon, Murillo rose to become the city's most beloved painter and a founder of its drawing academy.

His Immaculate Conceptions — the Virgin on a crescent moon in spirals of cherubim — became Catholicism's default vision of the doctrine.
