Xu Beihong

1895–1953 · Galloping horses — Chinese ink schooled in Paris anatomy

A poor seal-carver's son from Jiangsu, Xu Beihong drew for rice before scholarships carried him to Paris's École des Beaux-Arts.

His galloping horses — ribcages of wet ink, manes flying in dry-brush — became modern China's most reproduced images, painted by the hundred during the war years and sold abroad to fund relief.
