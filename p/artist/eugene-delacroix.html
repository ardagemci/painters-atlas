Eugène Delacroix

1798–1863 · Colour as passion — the lion of Romanticism

Possibly the natural son of Talleyrand — Paris certainly whispered so — Delacroix cultivated a dandy's reserve over a furnace of ambition.

Delacroix unleashed colour and movement against Ingres's cult of line: massacres, lion hunts, Byron's heroes, and Liberty herself striding the barricade — tricolour up, musket bayoneted.
