Jan van Eyck

c. 1390–1441 · 'As I can' — oil paint perfected, reality outdone

Van Eyck perfected oil glazing so completely that legend credited him with inventing paint itself. Light travels into his panels and comes back changed — brocade you can rustle, mirrors holding whole rooms. Northern painting's entire tradition of total observation starts at his easel.

He did not invent oil paint, but he perfected it so completely that legend credited him anyway: translucent glazes building light inside the picture, brocade thread by thread, a mirror reflecting a whole room in a circle the size of a plum.
