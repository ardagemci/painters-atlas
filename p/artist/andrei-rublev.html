Andrei Rublev

c. 1360s–1428 · The Trinity — three angels, one cup, a millennium of prayer distilled

Of the monk Andrei Rublev history keeps a handful of chronicle lines: he worked beside Theophanes the Greek on Moscow's Annunciation Cathedral in 1405, frescoed Vladimir's Dormition Cathedral in 1408, and died at the Andronikov Monastery around 1428.

His Trinity icon — Abraham's three angelic visitors seated about a single chalice, painted in tones critics compare to 'colour heard as music' — became the standard against which all Orthodox icon painting is measured.
