Gustave Doré

1832–1883 · The illustrator of the century — Dante, Quixote and London, engraved into every imagination

A Strasbourg prodigy who never took a drawing lesson, Doré was hired at fifteen by the Paris caricature press as its youngest paid artist and simply never stopped: over a hundred illustrated books, vast salon canvases, watercolours and late sculpture.

When publishers balked at his deluxe Dante Inferno folio in 1861, he financed it himself — it sold out within weeks, and the begging letters followed.
