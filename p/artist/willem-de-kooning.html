Willem de Kooning

1904–1997 · 'Flesh was the reason oil paint was invented'

A Rotterdam working-class kid trained in commercial art, de Kooning stowed away on a freighter to America in 1926 with no English and total confidence.

He refused the purity wars: 'Flesh was the reason oil paint was invented.' Woman I — two years, hundreds of scrapings, a grinning goddess-monster — scandalised abstraction's priests by being a figure at all.
