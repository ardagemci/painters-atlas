Giuseppe Arcimboldo

1526–1593 · Faces built from fruit — the emperor's favourite visual pun

A Milanese painter of conventional church windows and frescoes, Arcimboldo moved to the Habsburg courts of Vienna and Prague, serving three emperors for twenty-five years as portraitist, festival designer and procurer of wonders for the imperial cabinet of curiosities.

His composite heads — Spring assembled from blossoms, the Librarian from books, Emperor Rudolf II as the harvest god Vertumnus built entirely of produce — delighted a court obsessed with riddles and natural marvels.
