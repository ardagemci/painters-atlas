Jasper Johns

b. 1930 · Flags, targets, numbers — things the mind already knows, made strange

Raised in small-town South Carolina by relatives after his parents' divorce, Johns reached New York at twenty-two, destroyed nearly everything he'd made, and began again with a dream: he would paint a large American flag.

Flag (1954–55) — the Stars and Stripes in encaustic over shredded newspaper — is both a flag and a painting of one, a koan that rerouted art toward the conceptual.
