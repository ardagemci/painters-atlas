Keith Haring

1958–1990 · Radiant babies in the subway — art for everybody, drawn at full sprint

A church-raised kid from Kutztown, Pennsylvania who learned cartooning from his father, Haring hit New York's School of Visual Arts in 1978 and its club scene simultaneously.

Spotting empty black ad panels in the subway, he began chalking — quick, linear, joyous: barking dogs, radiant babies, dancing figures — up to forty drawings a day, arrested repeatedly, adored universally.
