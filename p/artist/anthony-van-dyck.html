Anthony van Dyck

1599–1641 · The man who taught aristocracy how to look effortless

An Antwerp silk merchant's son and child prodigy, Van Dyck was running his own studio in his teens and serving as Rubens's chief assistant by nineteen.

Van Dyck invented the aristocratic portrait as we know it: elongated figures, liquid silk, hands of impossible refinement, and an air of melancholy command.
