Francisco Goya

1746–1828 · From royal tapestries to the Black Paintings — reason's sleep, documented

No artist travelled further in one lifetime: sunny tapestry cartoons to the Black Paintings on his own dining-room wall. Goya invented war art without heroes, satire without safety, and nightmare without mythology. Modern art's conscience — and its insomnia — start with him.

No artist ever travelled further within one lifetime: sunny Rococo tapestry cartoons, glittering royal portraits that flatter no one, then the Caprichos' donkey-doctors and witches, the unflinching Disasters of War etchings, and finally Saturn eating his child on his own dining-room wall.
