Raphael

1483–1520 · Grace itself — the prince of painters, gone at thirty-seven

For three centuries 'painting well' simply meant painting like Raphael. The prodigy from Urbino absorbed Leonardo's smoke and Michelangelo's muscle, added a grace neither possessed, and died at thirty-seven having defined harmony itself — so completely that rebels from the Pre-Raphaelites onward had to name themselves against him.

Summoned by Pope Julius II at twenty-five, Raphael frescoed the papal apartments while Michelangelo painted the Sistine ceiling next door.
