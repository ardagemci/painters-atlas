Salvador Dalí

1904–1989 · Melting clocks and a waxed moustache — the dream rendered in miniature precision

Dalí rendered dreams with a miniaturist's precision — soft watches, crutch-propped flesh, beaches of guilt — and marketed the unconscious better than anyone alive. Expelled from Surrealism for excess, he correctly replied that he was Surrealism. The mustache was load-bearing.

His 'paranoiac-critical method' — systematic self-induced delirium — produced the canon of dream painting: soft watches, spider-legged elephants, lobster telephones, all executed with Vermeer-grade technique he called 'hand-painted dream photographs.' Expelled from the Surrealist group in 1934 for politics and showmanship, he answered: 'I myself am Surrealism.'
