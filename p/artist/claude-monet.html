Claude Monet

1840–1926 · Impression, sunrise to water lilies — sixty years chasing light

Monet chased fugitive light for sixty years and invented the modern series along the way — the same haystack, cathedral, pond, repainted as the hour turned. Impressionism carries his painting's name by accident and his stubbornness by design. The water lilies end where abstraction begins.

His harbour sketch Impression, Sunrise lent the whole movement its mocking name.
