Lyubov Popova

1889–1924 · Painterly architectonics — then fabric by the metre for the Revolution

Born to a wealthy merchant family near Moscow, Popova studied with Le Fauconnier and Metzinger in Paris, absorbed Tatlin's studio culture at home, and became the Russian avant-garde's most rigorous builder of pictures.

Her Painterly Architectonics stack tilted planes of red, black and ochre like girders under stress — abstraction with a structural engineer's conviction.
