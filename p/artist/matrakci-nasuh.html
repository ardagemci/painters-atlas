Matrakçı Nasuh

c. 1480–c. 1564 · The polymath who painted cities like maps that dream

Recruited through the Ottoman palace system, Nasuh of Visoko served Süleyman the Magnificent as mathematician, historian, master swordsman and inventor of a martial game with cudgels — the 'matrak' that gave him his name.

Chronicling Süleyman's Baghdad campaign, he invented a genre: topographic miniatures that portray each halting-place — Istanbul, Tabriz, Aleppo — as a jewel-bright city of mosques, bridges and gardens seen from everywhere at once, with not a single human figure.
