Fernand Léger

1881–1955 · Cubism with engine oil — the machine age, painted in primary gears

A Normandy cattle-merchant's son and architectural draughtsman, Léger reached Cubism via Cézanne — critics mocked his cylinders as 'tubism'.

The City made modern life — billboards, scaffolds, traffic — painting's subject at mural scale; his figures became magnificent machine-parts with souls, workers and acrobats assembled like engines in primary colours.
