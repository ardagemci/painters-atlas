Tsuguharu Foujita

1886–1968 · Montparnasse's bowl-cut dandy — milk-white skin drawn with a sumi line

Son of an Imperial Army doctor-general, Foujita left Tokyo's academy for 1913 Paris and became Montparnasse's most recognisable figure: bowl haircut, round glasses, gold earrings, a wristwatch tattooed on his wrist.

His 'grand fond blanc' — a secret milky ground whose recipe died with him — let him draw on oil paint with sumi-ink line: nudes like warm porcelain, cats rendered hair by hair, the only Japanese painter the École de Paris treated as a first-rank star.
