Remedios Varo

1908–1963 · Alchemy in the kitchen — clockwork pilgrims grinding starlight

Daughter of an Andalusian hydraulic engineer who taught her technical drawing — the precision never left — Varo passed through Madrid's academy (Dalí's cohort), anarchist Barcelona and Surrealist Paris with the poet Benjamin Péret, fleeing to Mexico City in 1941.

Her small panels are machines of the soul, painted with miniaturist exactness: an owl-woman distilling birds from violin-light, towers where girls embroider the world into being, a moon fed porridge of ground stars.
