Pablo Picasso

1881–1973 · The century in one career — blue, rose, shattered, remade

Picasso is the century in one career: blue grief, rose circuses, Cubism's earthquake, Guernica's scream, and fifty thousand works of restless everything. He didn't have a style; he had appetites. Every argument about what art is allowed to do passes through him.

The melancholy Blue and circus-tinted Rose periods would alone have secured a reputation; instead came Les Demoiselles d'Avignon — five fractured nudes that cracked five centuries of perspective — and, with Braque, Cubism itself.
