Hilma af Klint

1862–1944 · Abstraction's secret first mover — sealed by her own will for decades

Af Klint painted wall-sized pure abstraction in 1907 — years before Kandinsky, Malevich or Mondrian — on commission, she said, from spirit guides, then sealed the work away for decades. When the crates finally opened, abstraction needed a new birth certificate. Art history is still filling in the paperwork.

From 1906 — years before Kandinsky or Mondrian — she painted monumental abstractions: the ten-foot Ten Largest swirl with spirals, letters and biomorphic blooms charting life's ages.
