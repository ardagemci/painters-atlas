Helen Frankenthaler

1928–2011 · Mountains and Sea at twenty-three — colour poured straight into the canvas

A New York State Supreme Court judge's daughter raised on Park Avenue, Frankenthaler had the confidence of her upbringing and the work ethic to justify it.

In 1952, aged twenty-three, she thinned oils to washes and poured them onto raw unprimed canvas on the floor: Mountains and Sea.
