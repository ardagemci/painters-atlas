Giovanni Battista Piranesi

1720–1778 · Imaginary prisons, printed forever — Rome bigger than Rome

A Venetian stonemason's son trained as an architect, Piranesi reached Rome at twenty and never recovered: 'These speaking ruins have filled my spirit.' He built almost nothing — one church — but etched over a thousand colossal plates, ran a famous print shop and antiquities business near the Spanish Steps, and worked until his death at fifty-eight.

His Views of Rome supplied Europe's imagination with a city grander, darker and taller than reality — Goethe found the real Rome a disappointment by comparison.
